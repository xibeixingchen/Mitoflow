# Plant Mitochondrial Genome Structure

## Overview

Plant mitochondrial genomes are characterized by large size, extensive repeat content, and dynamic structural rearrangements. They can form multiple configurations through recombination.

## Genome Size

| Group | Typical Size | Range |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| Angiosperms | 200-700 kb | 66 kb (Viscum) to 11.3 Mb (Silene) |
| Gymnosperms | 1-2 Mb | Relatively conserved |
| Bryophytes | 100-250 kb | Smaller, more compact |
| Algae | 50-150 kb | Minimal gene content |

## Structural Organization

### Master Circle Model
- Single circular molecule containing all genetic information
- Recombination between repeats generates sub-genomic circles
- Original model, now considered oversimplified

### Multipartite Model
- Multiple circular molecules coexist
- Sub-genomic circles from recombination
- Stoichiometry varies between molecules
- Current consensus model

### Linear Molecules
- Some evidence for linear molecules
- May represent recombination intermediates
- Or artifacts of extraction/preparation

## Repeat Content

### Types of Repeats
| Type | Size | Frequency | Role |
|------|------|-----------|------|
| Large repeats | >1 kb | Common | Recombination, isomerization |
| Small repeats | 100-1000 bp | Very common | Illegitimate recombination |
| Microrepeats | <100 bp | Ubiquitous | Recombination hotspots |

### Recombination
- **Homologous recombination**: Between large repeats (>1 kb)
- **Illegitimate recombination**: Between small repeats
- Generates structural heteroplasmy
- Substoichiometric molecules (sublimons)

## Gene Content

### Core Genes (conserved across land plants)
- ~24 protein-coding genes
- 3 rRNA genes (26S, 18S, 5S)
- ~20 tRNA genes (some nuclear-encoded)

### Variable Genes
- rps genes (some transferred to nucleus)
- ORFs (some CMS-associated)
- Intronic ORFs (maturases)

## Gene Transfer to Nucleus

- Ongoing process over evolutionary time
- Most ribosomal protein genes transferred
- Some tRNA genes transferred
- Requires acquisition of targeting peptides
- Rate varies across lineages

## Introns

### Group I Introns
- Self-splicing
- Found in cox1, rns, rnl
- Some have ORFs (homing endonucleases)

### Group II Introns
- Self-splicing (lariat mechanism)
- Found in many PCGs
- Some require trans-splicing
- Maturase ORFs assist splicing

## Implications for Assembly

1. **Repeat content**: Causes assembly fragmentation
2. **Heteroplasmy**: Multiple configurations in one individual
3. **NUMTs**: Nuclear copies of mitochondrial DNA
4. **Size variation**: Dramatic across species
5. **Coverage**: Repeats show elevated coverage
