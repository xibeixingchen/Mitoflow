# Plant Mitochondrial Genome Biology

## Overview

Plant mitochondrial genomes are among the most complex organellar genomes in eukaryotes. Unlike animal mitochondria (~16kb, 37 genes), plant mitochondria range from 200kb to 2Mb and contain 50-60 genes.

## Key Features

### Size and Structure
- Typical size: 200kb-2Mb (some species exceed 10Mb)
- Circular molecule with frequent recombination
- Low gene density: large intergenic spacers
- Rich in repetitive sequences (often >30% of genome)

### Gene Content
- **Protein-coding genes (~35)**: respiratory chain subunits, ribosomal proteins, cytochrome c biogenesis
- **tRNA genes (~20)**: some imported from nucleus
- **rRNA genes (3)**: 18S, 5S, 26S (note: 26S is split in some species)

### Respiratory Chain Complexes
| Complex | Genes | Function |
|---------|-------|----------|
| I (NADH dehydrogenase) | nad1-9, nad4L | NADH oxidation |
| III (bc1) | cob | Electron transfer |
| IV (cytochrome c oxidase) | cox1-3 | Electron transfer to O2 |
| V (ATP synthase) | atp1, atp4, atp6, atp8, atp9 | ATP synthesis |
| Cytochrome c biogenesis | ccmB, ccmC, ccmFC, ccmFN, ccmFH | Heme attachment |
| Ribosomal proteins | rpl2, rpl5, rpl10, rpl16, rps1-4, rps7, rps10-14 | Translation |

## Unique Features

### RNA Editing
- C-to-U RNA editing is extensive (hundreds of sites per genome)
- Creates start codons, removes premature stop codons
- Affects phylogenetic analyses: DNA vs protein-level trees differ
- Mediated by PPR (pentatricopeptide repeat) proteins

### Trans-splicing
- Some genes are split across the genome
- **nad1**: 5 exons, trans-spliced
- **nad2**: 5 exons, trans-spliced in some species
- **nad5**: 4 exons, trans-spliced

### Multi-configuration Genomes
- Repeat-mediated recombination creates structural heterogeneity
- Substoichiometric molecules persist
- Implications for assembly and analysis
