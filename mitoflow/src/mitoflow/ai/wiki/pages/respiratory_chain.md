# Plant Mitochondrial Respiratory Chain

## Overview

Plant mitochondria have a branched respiratory chain with multiple terminal oxidases. The standard complexes I-IV plus ATP synthase are encoded by both mitochondrial and nuclear genes.

### Features
- Largest complex (~45 subunits in plants)
- Contains FMN, iron-sulfur clusters
- Plants have additional subunits not in mammals

- F1-F0 type ATP synthase
- Plants have additional subunits

## Alternative Oxidase (AOX)

- Nuclear-encoded terminal oxidase
- Bypasses complexes III and IV
- Not coupled to ATP synthesis
- Important for stress response
- Gene: AOX1, AOX2 (nuclear)

## NADH Dehydrogenase (Type II)

- Nuclear-encoded alternative NADH dehydrogenases
- Bypasses complex I
- Not coupled to proton pumping
- Genes: NDA1, NDA2, NDB1-4 (nuclear)

## Implications for Annotation

- Complex I genes are the most numerous in the mitogenome
- Many are trans-spliced (nad1, nad2, nad3, nad4, nad5, nad6)
- Some have RNA editing at start/stop codons
- cob and cox genes are reliable seed genes for assembly
